Auditum Memoria

Auditum Memoria is an Experimental Pre-Litigation Intelligence Lab dedicated to applied research in the structuring of memory, evidence and organizational reality.

The laboratory develops and experiments with methods designed to transform fragmented experiences, conflicts, narratives or weak signals into structures that are coherent, transmissible, actionable — and, through sustained analysis, generative of meaning that did not previously exist in the raw material.

Its work focuses primarily on the pre-litigation, pre-arbitration and pre-resolution spaces, where the facts already exist but have not yet found their definitive form.

Some of these methods remain experimental. Others progressively evolve into frameworks, concepts or artefacts that are gradually tested and refined through real-world situations.

Before being a framework or a doctrine, Auditum Memoria is first and foremost a laboratory of thought. It draws on already-established fields — game theory, sociology of law, anthropology of power, social psychology, epistemology, rhetoric — which it reformulates and recontextualizes in its own way, without depending on any institutional funding that would limit its candor. It is from this soil that concepts are born. Only afterwards do these concepts take shape as a framework, as deployment doctrines, as concrete case applications — whose value only reality itself can confirm or deny.

Auditum Memoria constitutes the research and development environment behind several works dedicated to narrative, evidentiary and strategic structuring.
